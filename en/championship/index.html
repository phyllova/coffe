WHAT TO EXPECT

JOIN US
The Cupping Room

Visitors will get to see the judging in action. In this room, the sensory judges conduct the cupping sessions for both Sane’ Al Gahwa Championship and the Gahwa Roaster Award. The judges will be , assessing the quality of each contestant's Gahwa through a rigorous evaluation of the coffee’s roast colour, taste balance (aromatics, acidity, bitterness, and flavor), defects, faults, and taints.

Live Competitions

Visitors can watch the competitions live at different stages; from Gahwa roasting on open fires using traditional utensils to competitors unleashing their creativity to brew the best Gahwa using only the ingredients provided in a mystery box.

Celebrate the Winners

The Gahwa Championships will conclude with an award ceremony at Al Hosn Festival to recognize the new winners of the competitions.

Abdulla Al Yammahi

Judge, HeritageSaudi Arabia

Abdulla Al Yammahi, also known as Abdullah Al Hamour, is a researcher, author and heritage expert in the United Arab Emirates. Among his most notable works are “Adab Sab Al Gahwa fi Al Emarat 95 Adaban” & “Sin3 Al Majalis fi Al Emarat”. He has a bachelor’s degree in Law and a Diploma in Cultural Heritage and has published many articles in the Dirasat Magazine of the Emirate’s Writers Union.
Al Yammahi is a General Supervisor in the Department of Majalis and Community Development Projects in the Emirate of Fujairah. His knowledge and expertise were integral in the inscription of Arabic Coffee (Gahwa) on the UNESCO Representative List of the Intangible Cultural Heritage of Humanity in 2015. Al Yammahi has been a guest heritage lecturer and speaker at several government entities, universities and schools in the UAE, as well as a guest speaker on television and radio shows, talking about Emirati customs and traditions. He also has a monthly column in Marawed Magazine, an initiative from Sharjah Institute for Heritage.

Fatima Al Mughanni

Heritage JudgeUnited Arab Emirates

Fatima Al Mughanni is a certified instructor in the United Arab Emirates Sane Programme, an initiative focussed on traditional etiquette of the Council of Ministers under the management of the Ministry of Culture and Knowledge Development, and the Ministry of Community Development. Al Mughanni has a Bachelor's and a Master's degree in education and psychology and has served as a judge at the Dubai World Hospitality Championships. She has also delivered many training courses and lectures on traditional etiquette and hospitality.
Al Mughanni is member of more than 50 social and charitable societies, both inside and outside the United Arab Emirates, and has served as a founder and member of many community associations including: Rewaq Ousha bint Hussein Cultural Centre; the Emirates Association for Volunteer; and Emirates Red Crescent.

His continuous participation in his interesting novels was accompanied by his attractive style through his presentation and the poem through the audio and visual media through multiple television channels. Abdullah Bin Kulayb

Heritage JudgeSaudi Arabia

Bin Kulayb brings with him his knowledge of Gahwa traditions from central Arabia with the understanding of regional differences. He fell in love with different cultures and their impact on the lives of peoples, he grasped their intellectual and heritage products and tried to understand them in order to deepen interdependence and enhance coexistence between civilizations. He travelled across the world to discover sources of coffee and its associated cultural heritage, to countries such as Ethiopia, Costa Rica, Cuba, and Mexico. He was a judge at the Gahwa Championships in 2019 as well Darb Al Finjal in Saudi Arabia in 2022.
